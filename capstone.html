Capstone Project

Culminate your 10-week journey with an original quantitative finance project. Build, present, and showcase your skills to peers and industry professionals.

Capstone Format

The capstone project is the culmination of the 10-week curriculum, allowing you to apply all learned concepts to a real-world quantitative finance problem.

Team Formation

Form teams that combine complementary skills and interests

Team Size

Teams should consist of 2-4 members. Smaller teams allow for deeper individual contribution, while larger teams enable more complex projects.

Team Composition

Ideal teams combine members with different strengths: quantitative modeling, programming, data analysis, and presentation skills.

Formation Process

Teams are formed during Week 5, after members have had time to get to know each other and identify complementary skills. Faculty advisors can help facilitate team formation.

Project Tracks

Choose a track that aligns with your interests and career goals

üìä

Factor Research

Develop and test alpha factors using fundamental, technical, or alternative data sources. Build factor models and evaluate factor performance.

Key Topics

  • Factor construction and engineering
  • Factor analysis and performance metrics
  • Multi-factor models
  • Factor decay and turnover analysis
  • Alternative data integration

Required Skills

Statistical analysis, data processing, Python/R programming, financial data APIs

‚öñÔ∏è

Statistical Arbitrage

Build pairs trading or mean reversion strategies. Implement cointegration models, signal generation, and execution algorithms.

Key Topics

  • Pairs trading and cointegration
  • Mean reversion strategies
  • Signal generation and filtering
  • Execution algorithms
  • Risk management for stat arb

Required Skills

Time series analysis, correlation analysis, backtesting frameworks, statistical modeling

üìà

Volatility & Options

Model volatility dynamics, price options, or develop options trading strategies. Work with volatility surfaces and Greeks.

Key Topics

  • Volatility modeling (GARCH, stochastic vol)
  • Options pricing models
  • Volatility surfaces and smiles
  • Options trading strategies
  • Greeks and risk management

Required Skills

Stochastic processes, numerical methods, options theory, volatility modeling

üéØ

Portfolio Optimizer

Build portfolio optimization systems. Implement risk parity, factor-based optimization, or advanced constraint handling.

Key Topics

  • Modern Portfolio Theory
  • Risk parity and minimum variance
  • Factor-based optimization
  • Transaction costs and constraints
  • Robust optimization methods

Required Skills

Optimization theory, covariance estimation, constraint programming, portfolio analytics

Deliverables

All projects must include the following components

Week 5

Project Proposal

Submit a 2-3 page proposal outlining your research question, methodology, data sources, and expected outcomes. Include team member roles.

Week 7

Progress Report

Mid-point check-in with preliminary results, challenges encountered, and updated timeline. Include code repository link.

Week 10

Final Deliverables

  • Code Repository: Well-documented GitHub repository with all code, data processing, and analysis
  • Written Report: 8-12 page technical report with methodology, results, and conclusions
  • Presentation: 10-15 minute presentation with slides for the Capstone Showcase
  • Demo/Visualization: Interactive demo or visualization of your strategy/results

Judging Rubric

Projects are evaluated by faculty and industry professionals using the following criteria

30%

Technical Quality

  • Rigor of quantitative methods
  • Code quality and documentation
  • Statistical validity
  • Implementation correctness
25%

Innovation & Originality

  • Novel approach or methodology
  • Creative problem-solving
  • Unique data sources or applications
  • Contribution to the field
20%

Results & Impact

  • Quality of results and analysis
  • Performance metrics (if applicable)
  • Practical applicability
  • Robustness testing
15%

Presentation & Communication

  • Clarity of presentation
  • Visual quality of slides
  • Ability to explain complex concepts
  • Q&A handling
10%

Documentation & Reproducibility

  • Quality of written report
  • Code documentation
  • Reproducibility of results
  • Data and methodology transparency
